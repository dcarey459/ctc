How do you get preapproved?

A lender can prequalify you to buy a house by assessing your income, assets and down payment. Then they will verify your financial information and submit your loan application to underwriting. A preapproval letter shows you’re a serious buyer. Getting the right mortgage is very important!


A fixed-rate conventional loan:

With this option, your interest rate is the same for the life of the loan. Protected from rising rates. Any other mortgage option is a bad idea. A 15-year term your mortgage payment will be higher with a 15-year term, but you’ll knock out your mortgage in half the time of a 30-year term and save thousands in interest. Strive for a monthly payment no more than 25% of your monthly take-home pay: This leaves room to achieve other goals like saving for retirement or putting money aside for your kid’s college fund. Once you count the cost on your new home, stick to it.
