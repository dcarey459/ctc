The salesperson is really one of the people whom you’ll meet who is the lowest person in the chain of the heirarchy of the sales team! Most dealerships are run by a series of managers who are ex-salespeople. The chain of command goes something like this:


  1. The Owner: bka, dealer principle. Like Roth Mills Shever Le'. He is the moneyman!

  2. The General Mgr-GM: is hired by the owner to oversee the entire dealership, managers, sales department, finance department, service department, body shop, detail and cleanup department, and business office.

  3. General Sales Mgr-GSM: runs the managers and governs the sales department.

  4. Sales Mgr: runs the sales force and trains the salespeople.

  5. F & I Mgr: arranges financing with banks and lending institutions.

  6. Used car mgr: Appraises trade ins and buys used cars for dealerships to sell.

  7. Closer: veteran salesperson or even a manager whose skilled in persuading you to buy.

  8. Salesperson: trained to demonstrate, drive, and sell automobiles to consumers.

This team is well trained. They study videos, take test, listen to audio and computer training, and attend seminars to equip them with the information necessary to successfully sell automobiles.

THE SALESPERSON’S STRATEGY

Here are the steps to selling you a car:


  • The Smile and the Handshake
  • Information Interview
  • Pick One
  • Walk around Demonstration
  • Test Drive Validation
  • Proposal to Purchase (4 square!)
  • Credit Report and other Paperwork
  • The Dance; Negotiating the numbers!
  • The Service Walk
  • T.O. or turn over the sale to the closer!

The Smile & Handshake: This first step is designed to gain control over you. The salesperson will attempt to establish common ground with you and create a bond of trust. Now that your guard is down, the next series of moves are strategically developed as a well-planned chess match. The goal is check and mate, game over!
