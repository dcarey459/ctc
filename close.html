Close the Deal!


Eureka! You're now a home owner!

The last step in this house-buying process is to close on your new home! Before you get the keys you have to complete all the paperwork. You were given a copy of your closing documents ahead of time to review so there are no sudden surprises on the day of closing. Most of the time you're likely to pay for:


  1. Your Homeowner’s insurance
  2. The Homeowner’s association fees (if applicable)
  3. The prorated property tax
  4. The closing costs

If you don't understand something like the terminonolgy as you go over the paperwork, SAY SOMETHING NOW! You have that right and should know exactly what you’re signing and agreeing to. This is probably the largest investment of your life. You need to "Count the Cost!" Once you're cool and sign all the docs, you’re a homeowner. Congratulations! Bless God its done!

Home Sweet Home!

Find the Right
Real Estate Agent


Do a lot of research on your own.

But you need an expert when it comes to actually finding and securing your home. A buyer’s agent can help you tremendously. They can help you find a house before it hits the market. And your agent will negotiate on your behalf so you don’t pay a penny more than the home is worth.

How much should you pay a buyer’s agent?

How does nothing sound? In most cases, the seller pays your real estate agent’s fees. Using a buyer’s agent is free to you. So have a true pro in your corner. Not all agents are created equal. Don’t work with an amateur. You want experience!
Buyer’s agent requirements:


  1. Full-time real estate experience for at least two years
  2. Great communication skills
  3. Makes you feel like you’re their only client
  4. Long list of sold homes every year
  5. Exceptional experience in your local market
