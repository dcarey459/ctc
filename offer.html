Submit an Offer


What’s included in your offer?

Your agent will help you to submit an good offer. Sometimes you're in a bidding war with other buyers. Stay cool, things like this happen. But being preapproved and having a flexible closing date can help make your offer more appealing. What your purchase agreement includes:


  1. The buyer and sellers information
  2. The property address
  3. The purchase price, lending institution information and your down payment amount
  4. Your earnest money deposit
  5. Items that are staying with the property. (like appliances etc.)
  6. Contingencies such as the home inspection, the appraisal. And also the final mortgage approval
  7. The closing date

Negotiations are sometimes challenging. But both parties want to feel their winning. They want to sell and you want to buy. Compromise on small stuff. In the movie the "Gambler" the song says "You got to know when to hold em, know when to fold em, know when to walk away, and know when to run!"




Find the Right
Real Estate Agent


Do a lot of research on your own.

But you need an expert when it comes to actually finding and securing your home. A buyer’s agent can help you tremendously. They can help you find a house before it hits the market. And your agent will negotiate on your behalf so you don’t pay a penny more than the home is worth.

How much should you pay a buyer’s agent?

How does nothing sound? In most cases, the seller pays your real estate agent’s fees. Using a buyer’s agent is free to you. So have a true pro in your corner. Not all agents are created equal. Don’t work with an amateur. You want experience!
Buyer’s agent requirements:


  1. Full-time real estate experience for at least two years
  2. Great communication skills
  3. Makes you feel like you’re their only client
  4. Long list of sold homes every year
  5. Exceptional experience in your local market
